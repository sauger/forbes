Panel Ⅰ: Industries with Biggest Investment Value and Development Potential in China
　　The structural imbalance of China’s economy has been laid bare by the financial crisis. The export-oriented economy leads to over-reliance on overseas markets. As a result, the falling international demand has hit China’s export trade and a considerable part of export service industry very hard. Indeed, industrial restructuring has never been more urgent. Industries such as renewable energy, new materials, energy conservation, environmental protection, bio-pharmaceutical, consumer goods & retail and TMT have drawn the attention of investors and start-up entrepreneurs. The rise of emerging industries without state monopoly will facilitate the development of SMEs and industrial restructuring and upgrading. How can we further product R & D and commercialization (which will have a huge impact on future economic competiveness)? How can we seize investment opportunities in these new industries? Successful investors will share their experience in making investment choices.

Panel Ⅱ: New Ways for Industrial Innovation
　　Innovation is the soul of enterprise. Industrial innovation is the highest level and the ultimate goal of corporate innovation. It marks a journey in which enterprises break their structural constraints and utilize innovation in technology, product and market, or a combination of all to transform existing industrial structure or create new industries. SMEs have become the source and driver of industrial innovation in China. They can only withstand rigorous tests with a deep commitment to innovation and adaptation. How do SMEs find new ways for industry innovation? How to find new factors, new customers, new channels and new products necessary for breakthroughs and development so as to enable innovation and development of the whole industry? Creative entrepreneurs will discuss with investors on how to open the door to industrial innovation together.
