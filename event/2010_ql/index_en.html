2010 Forbes China Best SME Investment and Development Forum
　　In responding to the financial crisis, the Chinese government adopted the most aggressive measures ever to support the economy in 2009, including an expansionary lending policy, a 4-trillion stimulus package, plans to revitalize ten industries, and a 500-billion structural tax cut program. The financial tsunami is still unraveling the global economy. Data indicates, however, China’s economy is bottoming out, though the country still has a long way to go to full recovery.
　　The most dynamic component in China’s economy, small and medium enterprises (SMEs) have gone through rigorous tests- cash flow stress, funding shortage, falling international demand, weak domestic demand, volatile raw material prices, rising labor cost, among others. Despite their major contributions to Corporate China’s size as well as the country’s job opportunities, sales, export and tax revenue, SMEs are still the disadvantaged relative to their large counterparts. To survive and develop, they must overcome more obstacles, let alone enjoy favorable bailout from the government......
